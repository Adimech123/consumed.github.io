About the Exhibition

One of the interviewee’s most vivid childhood memories is of time spent in their grandparents’ house in Marsa, especially in the small, greasy kitchen where their grandfather—a former Navy chef—cooked for the large family. Despite the clutter and the smells of frying and smoke, the kitchen was a place of creativity and warmth. The grandfather, a stern but fascinating figure, secretly shared his hidden whisky stash and offered quirky treats like sugar-coated fried ravioli and makeshift ice lollies made from ice cubes and bottle caps. The sensory richness of the house—from the turquoise kitchen walls and heavy cast iron pans to the smell of vegetable soup mingling with blooming purple roses—left a lasting impression. Over time, this early exposure to food and resourceful cooking deeply influenced the interviewee’s love for cuisine and shaped their appreciation for everyday creativity and family legacy..
